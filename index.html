Welcome! I am a botanist from Chile, currently doing a PhD at the University of Zurich in the lab of Dr. Michael Kessler . My research interest are fern ecophysiology and ecology, specifically how plant water-carbon relations changes with their environment, and better yet if I can study this from an evolutionary standpoint. This is what encourage me to study ferns since they are an ancient plant lineage that is severely understud ied.

Research topics

Water relations

One of the most limiting factors of ferns distribution is water availability. Understanding the physiological constrains and adaptations to drought found in ferns can provide a better insight into their evolutionary path.

Circadian rhythms

Angiosperms are known to use circadian rhythms as an adaptation to predict and anticipate environmental queues, but we do not know if this knowledge can be transfered to ferns. This is especially relevant due to the major differences between their physiologies.
